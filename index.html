Gabriel's theorem and Birational Geometry

Theorem(Gabriel). $X \simeq Y \iff \text{Coh}(X) \simeq \text{Coh}(Y)$

Theorem. $X \sim_\text{bir} Y \iff k(X) \simeq k(Y)$

SillyRemark:
$k(X) \simeq k(Y)$ $\iff$ $\text{Spec}(k(X)) \simeq \text{Spec}(k(Y))$ $\iff$ $\text{Coh}(\eta_X) \simeq \text{Coh}(\eta_Y)$

A few comments

  • Original proof works for noetherian schemes
  • Rosenberg(+Gabber) extend to quasi-separated schemes [see Brandenburg]
  • False for stacks: $\C \times \C \not\simeq B\Z/2$ [need to remember $\otimes$] #Tannakian
  • What about algebraic spaces? Tricky, as all proofs rely on locally ringed spaces.
