Gabriel's theorem and Birational Geometry
$X,Y$ algebraic varieties

Theorem(Gabriel) $X \simeq Y \iff \text{Coh}(X) \simeq \text{Coh}(Y)$

Theorem(C-Pirisi) $X \sim_c Y \iff \mathcal C^c(X) \simeq \mathcal C^c(Y)$

$\mathcal C^c(X) := \text{Coh}(X)/\text{Coh}_{\leq \dim X -c -1}(X)$

Theorem $X \sim_\text{bir} Y \iff k(X) \simeq k(Y)$

Comments on Gabriel
  • Original proof works for noetherian schemes
  • Rosenberg (+Gabber) extend to quasi-separated schemes (see Brandenburg)
  • For non-noetherian $X$, use $\text{QCoh}(X)$

    for noetherian $X$, $\text{QCoh}(X) = \text{IndCoh}(X)$

More comments
  • False for stacks: $\text{mod}(\CC \times \CC) \simeq \text{Coh}(B\Z/2)$
    (true if you remember $\otimes$)     #Tannakian
  • What about algebraic spaces? Tricky, as proofs rely on ringed spaces.
  • Gabriel-Rosenberg: $\mathcal A$ $\mapsto$ $\text{Spec}(\mathcal A)$ [ringed space]
    $\text{Spec}(\text{Coh}(X)) = X$
  • C.-Groechenig: $\mathcal A$ $\mapsto$ $\text{Pt}_{\mathcal A}$ [moduli space]
  • Result is true for (quasi-compact and separated) algebraic spaces

Quotient categories

Definition $\mathcal A$ abelian, $\mathcal S \subset \mathcal A$ subcategory. $\mathcal S$ is Serre if, given an exact sequence $A \to B \to C$, $A,C \in \mathcal S \iff B \in \mathcal S$

  • Can form quotient $\mathcal A / \mathcal S$
  • Satisfies universal property
  • Localization: $\mathcal A / \mathcal S = \mathcal A[q^{-1} | \text{ ker } q, \text{coker } q \in \mathcal S]$

$\text{Coh}_{\leq k}(X) \subset \text{Coh}(X)$ is Serre

$\C_k(X) := \text{Coh}(X) / \text{Coh}_{\leq k-1}(X)$

$\C^c(X) := \C_{\dim X - c}(X)$

$X$ irreducible variety

$\C^{\dim X}(X) = \text{Coh}(X)$

$\C^{0}(X) = \text{vect}(k(X))$

Meinhardt-Partsch study stability conditions on $D(\C^c(X))$

Facts With $X$ smooth, projective, and irreducible

  • $\C^c$ is of homological dimension $c$
  • $\text{Hom}_{\C^c}$ can be infinite-dimensional
  • There is no Serre functor on $D(\C^c(X))$, for $0 < c < \dim X$
